Celebrating Mother’s Day


Overview

The Mother’s Day Concert at Johannesburg Zoo is a premier annual lifestyle event hosted by Johannesburg City Parks and Zoo, celebrating and honoring mothers in a vibrant, family-friendly outdoor setting.

Set within one of Johannesburg’s most iconic green spaces, the event offers a unique combination of live music, relaxation, and nature, creating an unforgettable experience for attendees of all ages. Guests are invited to enjoy a carefully curated lineup of leading South African artists while spending quality time with family and loved ones in a safe, beautifully maintained environment.

This event has become a key highlight on the City’s events calendar, attracting thousands of visitors each year. It supports the organization’s mandate of activating public open spaces, encouraging community engagement, and promoting the sustainable use of recreational facilities.

Attendees can expect a full-day experience that includes live performances, picnic-style relaxation, food and beverage offerings, and access to designated areas within the Johannesburg Zoo.

The Mother’s Day Concert is positioned not only as an entertainment event but as a meaningful social experience that brings together music, culture, and community in celebration of one of the most important days of the year.

Event Details


The Mother’s Day Concert at Johannesburg Zoo is a flagship annual event hosted by Johannesburg City Parks and Zoo, bringing together music, nature, and community in a uniquely curated outdoor experience.

Held in celebration of Mother’s Day, the event provides a platform for families, friends, and communities to gather in a safe, scenic environment while enjoying live performances by some of South Africa’s most celebrated artists. The combination of a relaxed picnic-style setting and high-quality live entertainment makes this event a standout offering within the City’s events portfolio.

 

Background and Legacy

 

Over the years, the Mother’s Day Concert has grown into one of Johannesburg’s most anticipated lifestyle events, consistently attracting strong attendance and positive public engagement.

The event contributes to the strategic objectives of Johannesburg City Parks and Zoo by:

Activating public open spaces in a meaningful and sustainable manner

Encouraging community participation and social cohesion

Showcasing the Johannesburg Zoo as a premier recreational and events destination

Richard Cock

A renowned conductor and musician, Richard Cock worked in the music industry for most of his life. He is probably best known as the music director of the National Symphony Orchestra of South Africa but also held positions as a producer for the South African Broadcasting Corporation, director of music of St Mary’s cathedral, and artistic director of the Johannesburg Festival Orchestra. He currently serves on the management team of the Phoenix Orchestra. He conducts throughout the country and is equally at home in concert halls working with legendary soloist like Jullian Lloyd Webber and the late Pavarotti. It is generally believed that he has done more than anyone in South Africa to popularize classic music and still implement innovative ways to introduce music to new audiences, currently through the umbrella body Richard Cock Enterprises which covers various activities connected by music.

The Phoenix Orchestra

Phoenix Orchestra was formed after the COVID pandemic when concerts relying on large numbers of people sitting in close proximity indoors were no longer an option. The Orchestra arose from a need to take orchestras into communities – private gardens, churches, schools and other open public spaces – so that the public could continue to experience classical music, enabling musicians to continue making music in addition to earning some much-needed income. The principles of the Phoenix Co Orchestra are to promote diversity, transparency within the sector, accessibility, and the love of classical music. During 2021 the orchestra performed across the province of Gauteng, bringing live music out of the concert halls and into various communities. Since then, the orchestra has gone from strength to strength, continuing its community concerts, many of which were repeat requests from schools or churches and adding larger events such as RMB Starlight Classics, and adding concerts with the Symphony Choir of Johannesburg to its growing list of performances.

Event Guidelines

Please follow all event rules, safety protocols, and entry requirements.

The following guidelines have been put in place to ensure a safe, enjoyable, and well-managed experience for all attendees at the Mother’s Day Concert 2026.

All visitors are required to comply with these rules, which are aligned with Johannesburg Zoo regulations and applicable City of Johannesburg by-laws. Failure to adhere to these guidelines may result in refusal of entry or removal from the event.


Entry and Access Requirements

  • A valid ticket (digital or printed) must be presented at entry.
  • All attendees are subject to security screening, including bag checks.
  • No re-entry will be permitted without official accreditation.
  • Children under the age of 16 must be accompanied by a responsible adult.
  • Entry points and access routes must be used as directed by event staff
  • Children entering the kiddies’ play area will be issued with wristbands on which a parent or guardian’s contact number will be recorded for emergency purposes.


Permitted and Prohibited Items

Permitted

  • Picnic blankets and portable chairs
  • Personal food for picnic use
  • Sealed non-alcoholic beverages
  • Sunscreen, hats, and umbrellas


Prohibited

  • Weapons, sharp objects, or hazardous items
  • Drones or unauthorised recording equipment
  • Large sound equipment, such as speakers
  • Fires, gas braais, or open flames
  • Any items deemed unsafe by event management


Parking and Transport
Public parking is available at Upper Park Drive and the Military Museum 

  • Visitors are encouraged to use ride-sharing services such as Uber or Bolt (use Jan Smuts Entrance)
  • Traffic officials and signage must be followed at all times.
  • Parking is at the owner’s risk.


Safety and Security

  • Trained security personnel will be deployed throughout the venue
  • Emergency and medical services will be available on-site.
  • Clearly marked emergency assembly points will be in place.
  • Attendees must comply with all safety instructions from officials


General Event Rules

  • No littering. Use designated waste and recycling bins.
  • Respect all animal enclosures and restricted areas
  • Feeding of the animals is prohibited
  • No climbing on fences, structures, or staging areas
  • Noise levels must remain appropriate for the environment.
  • No unlawful or disruptive behaviour will be tolerated.
  • The organiser reserves the right to remove any individual who violates event rules


Alcohol Policy

  • Alcohol may only be purchased and consumed in designated areas
  • No alcohol may be brought into the venue
  • Proof of age may be required for alcohol purchases
  • Responsible consumption is expected at all times


Environmental Responsibility

  • Dispose of waste responsibly
  • Respect the natural environment
  • Support sustainability initiatives where applicable


Photography and Media

  • Photography and videography will take place during the event
  • By attending, visitors consent to the use of images for promotional purposes
  • Unauthorised commercial filming is not permitted


Emergency Procedures
In the event of an emergency:

  • Attendees must follow instructions from event officials and security personnel
  • Emergency exits and assembly points must be used as directed
  • Medical assistance will be available on-site
  • Clear communication systems will be in place to guide attendees where necessary.


Compliance and Regulations
The event will comply with all relevant regulations, including:

City of Johannesburg by-laws
Public safety and event regulations
Health and safety standards
Environmental management requirements

All operations will be conducted in accordance with approved permits and event plans.
